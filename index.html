Reflections on "Inevitable"

November 24 - Written by Jodie Chan

Dearest friends & family, I'm so very excited to share in just a few weeks on December 12 2020 - I'll be releasing my first ever single on Spotify, Tidal, Apple Music, and Google Play* (or wherever else you find your music!). [Now available for Pre-Save] My parents often say that I wrote songs as soon as I could talk, preferring sing-song speech to words until I started school. While my memory hasn't served me well in remembering how true that statement was - the many recordings they have of a hyperactive -younger Jodie dancing to ABBA, singing My Heart will Go On at my 4th birthday (ridiculous, I know), and rotating to Disney songs days on end make that scenario likely.

I'll insert a disclaimer here that I didn’t exhibit wild genius in my songs as they were apparently about taking a bath or improvised lullabies shower (yes, life was simpler then).> Those of you who've grown up with me will have heard me write songs here and there, perfectly happy to perform covers or in musicals as some fictional character on stage, but wildly reserved when it comes to performing as me and in my own words. This is all to say that I'm both shocked and not that in X days, I'll be sharing my first-ever single on public platforms. Shocked because I never thought I'd take my originals "seriously". Not shocked because, when looking back - maybe I've always had the opportunity and an army of people supporting me in this direction. You know who you are. Please meet a walking “thank you for the music” trope in the form of Jodie, today. A little taller, but maybe not all that different. (pic)
